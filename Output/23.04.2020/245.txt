                <h3 id="title">SR 16-13: <p>Imposition of Special Measures by the U.S. Department of the Treasury's Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN)</p></h3>
                
                <div class="row padded-row sr-letter__letterhead">
                	<div class="col-xs-12 col-sm-3">
                		<span class="icon icon__xxl icon-frb-logo-black"></span>
                	</div>
                	<div class="col-xs-12 col-sm-5">
                		BOARD OF GOVERNORS<br/>
                		OF THE FEDERAL RESERVE SYSTEM<br/>
                		WASHINGTON, D.C. 20551
                	</div>
                	<div class="col-xs-12 col-sm-4">
                		<p>
                		DIVISION OF BANKING<br/>
                		SUPERVISION AND REGULATION
                		</p>
    		
                        
                		<strong>SR 16-13</strong><br/>
                		<strong>July 29, 2016</strong>
                		<br/><strong>Revised November 13, 2017</strong>
                		<br/><strong>Attachment Reposted November 13, 2017</strong>
                	</div>
                </div>
                
                            
                <div class="row padded-row">
                    <div class="col-xs-12 col-md-12 col-sm-12"><strong><p><strong><em>On November 13, 2017, the List of Entities for Which FinCEN Has Imposed Special Measures Under Section&nbsp;311 of the USA&nbsp;PATRIOT Act (attachment) was updated to reflect current FinCEN measures.</em></strong></p></strong></div>
                </div>
                
                            
                <div class="row padded-row">
                    <div class="col-xs-12col-xs-12 col-md-12 col-sm-12"><strong><p>TO THE OFFICER IN CHARGE OF SUPERVISION AND APPROPRIATE SUPERVISORY AND EXAMINATION STAFF AT EACH FEDERAL RESERVE BANK AND EACH DOMESTIC AND FOREIGN BANKING ORGANIZATION SUPERVISED BY THE FEDERAL RESERVE</p></strong></div>
                </div>
                
                <div class="row padded-row sr-letter__subject">
                	<div class="col-xs-12 col-sm-2">SUBJECT:</div>
                	<div class="col-xs-12 col-sm-10"><p>Imposition of Special Measures by the U.S. Department of the Treasury's Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN)</p></div>
                </div>
                
                <div class="row panel panel-default panel-padded">
                	<p><strong>Applicability:</strong>&nbsp; This letter on special measures is directed to all covered financial institutions supervised by the Federal Reserve, regardless of asset size.</p>
                </div>
                
                <div class="row">
                	<p>The purpose of this letter is to advise Federal Reserve supervised institutions of the special measures imposed by the U.S. Department of the Treasury's Financial Crimes Enforcement Network&nbsp;(FinCEN) under section&nbsp;311 of the USA PATRIOT Act (31&nbsp;U.S.C.&nbsp;5318A).<a href="#_ftn1" id="_ftnref1" name="_ftnref1" title="Footnote 1"><sup>1</sup></a>&nbsp; Special measures create legal obligations for covered financial institutions with respect to certain jurisdictions, financial institutions, or international transactions of primary money laundering concern to the United States (collectively, "311&nbsp;entities").<a href="#_ftn2" id="_ftnref2" name="_ftnref2" title="Footnote 2"><sup>2</sup></a></p>

<p>Under section&nbsp;311 of the USA PATRIOT&nbsp;Act, FinCEN has authority to require covered financial institutions to take one or more of the following special measures with regard to a 311&nbsp;entity:</p>

<ol>
	<li>maintain records, file reports, or both, concerning the aggregate amount of transactions, or concerning each transaction of the entity;</li>
	<li style="margin-top: 2ex;">obtain and retain beneficial ownership information regarding U.S. accounts that involves the entity;</li>
	<li style="margin-top: 2ex;">identify and obtain information comparable to U.S. customer identification requirements regarding customers permitted to use, or whose transactions are routed through, payable through accounts of financial institutions involving the entity;</li>
	<li style="margin-top: 2ex;">identify and obtain information comparable to U.S. customer identification requirements regarding customers permitted to use, or whose transactions are routed through, correspondent accounts of financial institutions involving the entity; and</li>
	<li style="margin-top: 2ex;">prohibit, or impose conditions upon, the opening or maintaining in the United States of a correspondent account or payable-through account for the entity.</li>
</ol>

<p>Because the specific special measures imposed regarding 311&nbsp;entities can vary, covered financial institutions should refer to FinCEN's rulemaking or order pertaining to each 311&nbsp;entity for guidance regarding the nature, applicability, and scope of the imposed special measures.<a href="#_ftn3" id="_ftnref3" name="_ftnref3" title="Footnote 3"><sup>3</sup></a>&nbsp; Generally, the special measure most commonly imposed under section&nbsp;311 is the fifth special measure.&nbsp; The fifth special measure must be issued by rulemaking and prohibits all covered financial institutions from opening or maintaining a correspondent account in the United States for, or on behalf of, entities identified as a primary money laundering concern.&nbsp; The first four special measures may be imposed by order, without a final rulemaking.</p>

<p>Attached to this SR letter is a list of the 311&nbsp;entities for which a special measure is in place creating obligations on the part of covered financial institutions.&nbsp; This list will be updated periodically whenever FinCEN imposes a new special measure or removes, or changes an existing special measure.</p>

<p>Federal Reserve Banks are asked to distribute this letter to covered supervised domestic and foreign financial institutions as well as to supervisory and examination staff.&nbsp; For questions regarding these special measures, please contact <span style="white-space:nowrap">Koko Ives,</span> Manager, Bank Secrecy Act/Anti-Money Laundering Compliance Section <span style="white-space:nowrap">(202) 973-6163.</span>&nbsp; In addition, questions may be sent via the Board's public website.<a href="#_ftn4" id="_ftnref4" name="_ftnref4" title="Footnote 4"><sup>4</sup></a></p>
                </div>
