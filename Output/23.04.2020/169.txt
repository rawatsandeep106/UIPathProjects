                  <!-- Body of the letter -------------------- -->
                  <div class="letter_body">
                     <p>The Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System, together with the Federal Deposit Insurance <span class="nobreak">Corporation (FDIC),</span> the National Credit Union 
                     	<span class="nobreak">Administration (NCUA),</span> the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency, and the Office of Thrift Supervision, has issued the attached guidance 
                        addressing supervisory considerations related to bargain purchase gains and the impact such gains have on the application (licensing) approval process. Recent market conditions 
                        have contributed to an increase in bargain purchases, which has necessitated the issuance of this guidance. The attached guidance also highlights the accounting and reporting 
                        requirements unique to business combinations resulting in bargain purchase gains and FDIC- and NCUA-assisted acquisitions of failed institutions. </p>                
   
                     <p>Although this guidance principally focuses on bargain purchase gains, it is also relevant to business combinations in general. However, the guidance does not provide a 
                     	comprehensive discussion of all aspects of accounting for business combinations. The guidance does not add to or modify existing regulatory reporting requirements issued by the 
                        agencies or current accounting requirements under generally accepted accounting <span class="nobreak">principles (GAAP).</span> The principal sources of guidance on business 
                        combinations and related measurements under GAAP are found under Financial Accounting Standards <span class="nobreak">Board (FASB)</span> Accounting Standards 
                        <span class="nobreak">Codification (ASC)</span> <span class="nobreak">Topic 805,</span> <em>Business Combinations</em>, and ASC <span class="nobreak">Topic 820,</span> <em>Fair 
                        Value Measurements and Disclosures</em>.</p>
                     
                     <p>Reserve Banks are asked to distribute this letter to financial institutions supervised by the Federal Reserve in their districts, as well as to their own application, supervisory, 
                     	and examination staff. Questions on the attached guidance should be addressed to <span class="nobreak">Laurie Priest,</span> Manager, Accounting Policy and Disclosure, at 
                        <span class="nobreak">(202) 452-2750;</span> or <span class="nobreak">Matthew Kincaid,</span> Supervisory Financial Analyst, Accounting Policy and Disclosure, at 
                        <span class="nobreak">(202) 452-2028.</span></p>
