                  <div class="letter_body">
                  	<p>Attached is interagency guidance issued by the Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System and the other federal banking 
                     	agencies.<a href="#Footnote1" name="Footref1" title="Footnote 1"><sup>1</sup></a> This guidance reminds institutions of supervisory expectations on sound practices for managing 
                        risks associated with funding and credit concentrations arising from correspondent relationships (correspondent concentration 
                        risk).<a href="#Footnote2" name="Footref2" title="Footnote 2"><sup>2</sup></a> The guidance highlights the need for institutions to identify, monitor, and manage correspondent 
                        concentration risk on a standalone and organization-wide basis.  The guidance also reinforces the supervisory view that financial institutions should perform appropriate due 
                        diligence on all credit exposures to and funding transactions with other financial institutions.</p>
                     
                     <p>Reserve Banks are asked to distribute this letter to financial institutions supervised by the Federal Reserve, as well as to supervisory and examination staff. Questions 
                     	regarding this letter may be directed to <span class="nobreak">Barbara J. Bouchard,</span> Associate Director, at <span class="nobreak">(202) 452-3072;</span> or 
                        <span class="nobreak">Craig A. Luke,</span> Supervisory Financial Analyst, Supervisory Guidance and Procedures section, at <span class="nobreak">(202) 452-6409.</span> In 
                        addition, questions may be sent via the Board’s public website.<a href="#Footnote3" name="Footref3" title="Footnote 3"><sup>3</sup></a> </p>
