<td width=12% valign=top align=left><b>SUBJECT:</b></td>
<td width=88% align=left><font SIZE=3><b>Inspection Procedures for &quot;Section 20 Subsidiaries&quot;
</b></font></td>
</tr>
</table><br>
<font SIZE=3>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Enclosed is a copy of inspection procedures for the use
and guidance of Federal Reserve Bank staff in performing
inspections of nonbank subsidiaries that have approval to engage
in limited underwriting and dealing in bank &quot;ineligible&quot; debt or
equity securities.&nbsp;&nbsp;Those companies have come to be known as
&quot;Section 20 subsidiaries.&quot;
<p>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The procedures are all-inclusive and can be used for
the inspection of any Section 20 subsidiary -- regardless of the
subsidiary's scope of securities activities or whether it is part
of a domestic or foreign banking organization.&nbsp;&nbsp;The primary focus
of inspections will be to verify that policies and procedures are
in place to ensure compliance with Board &quot;firewall&quot; conditions;
verify adherence to the Board's revenue limitation on ineligible
securities activities; and evaluate the subsidiary's financial
condition and its impact on the consolidated organization.&nbsp;&nbsp;The
inspection procedures include separate instructions for both
initial and subsequent inspections, as well as supplemental
procedures to be used for conducting random or targeted tests
where necessary.&nbsp;&nbsp;It is intended that inspections avoid, to the
extent possible, duplicating efforts of the Section 20
subsidiary's securities self-regulatory organization.
<p>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
The Board has either modified or clarified its firewall
conditions on several occasions.&nbsp;&nbsp;Also, staff has reviewed and
evaluated certain operating procedures and practices for
compliance with the Board's Orders.&nbsp;&nbsp;As a result, the enclosed
materials include several appendices containing the various sets
of Board conditions and modifications thereto, as well as
explanatory comments.
<p>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As experience is gained with these new securities
activities by both bank holding companies and examiners, it is
likely that additional modifications of these procedures and/or
appendices will be necessary.&nbsp;&nbsp;Accordingly, it is expected that
these inspection procedures will be updated periodically, and
ultimately, be included in the Bank Holding Company Supervision
Manual.
<p>
