                <h3 id="title">SR 15-19: <p>Federal Reserve Supervisory Assessment of Capital Planning and Positions for Large and Noncomplex Firms</p></h3>
                
                <div class="row padded-row sr-letter__letterhead">
                	<div class="col-xs-12 col-sm-3">
                		<span class="icon icon__xxl icon-frb-logo-black"></span>
                	</div>
                	<div class="col-xs-12 col-sm-5">
                		BOARD OF GOVERNORS<br/>
                		OF THE FEDERAL RESERVE SYSTEM<br/>
                		WASHINGTON, D.C. 20551
                	</div>
                	<div class="col-xs-12 col-sm-4">
                		<p>
                		DIVISION OF BANKING<br/>
                		SUPERVISION AND REGULATION
                		</p>
    		
                        
                		<strong>SR 15-19</strong><br/>
                		<strong>December 18, 2015</strong>
                		
                		
                	</div>
                </div>
                
                
                            
                <div class="row padded-row">
                    <div class="col-xs-12col-xs-12 col-md-12 col-sm-12"><strong><p>TO THE OFFICER IN CHARGE OF SUPERVISION AT EACH FEDERAL RESERVE BANK</p></strong></div>
                </div>
                
                <div class="row padded-row sr-letter__subject">
                	<div class="col-xs-12 col-sm-2">SUBJECT:</div>
                	<div class="col-xs-12 col-sm-10"><p>Federal Reserve Supervisory Assessment of Capital Planning and Positions for Large and Noncomplex Firms</p></div>
                </div>
                
                <div class="row panel panel-default panel-padded">
                	<p><strong>Applicability: This guidance applies only to U.S. bank holding companies and intermediate holding companies of foreign banking organizations that have total consolidated assets of at least $50 billion but less than $250&nbsp;billion, have consolidated total on-balance sheet foreign exposure of less than $10 billion, and are not otherwise subject to the Federal Reserve's Large Institution Supervision Coordinating Committee (LISCC) framework (referred to as a "Large and Noncomplex Firm" in this letter).</strong></p>
                </div>
                
                <div class="row">
                	<p>The Federal Reserve is issuing this letter to explain its supervisory expectations for capital planning at large and noncomplex bank holding companies and intermediate holding companies of foreign banking organizations, consistent with the broad supervisory expectations set forth in SR letter 12-17/CA letter 12-14, "Consolidated Supervision Framework for Large Financial Institutions."<a href="#fn1" title="footnote 1"><sup>1</sup></a><a id="f1" name="f1"> </a>Capital is central to a firm's ability to absorb unexpected losses and continue to lend to creditworthy businesses and consumers. Therefore, a firm's processes for managing and allocating its capital resources are critical to its financial strength and resiliency, and also to the stability and effective functioning of the U.S. financial system. The attached guidance provides the Federal Reserve's core capital planning expectations for large and noncomplex firms, building upon the capital planning requirements in the Federal Reserve's capital plan rule and stress test rules.<a href="#fn2" title="footnote 2"><sup>2</sup></a><a id="f2" name="f2"> </a><sup>,<a href="#fn3" title="footnote 3">3</a><a id="f3" name="f3"> </a>&nbsp;</sup></p>

<p>The guidance outlines capital planning expectations for:</p>

<ul style=" margin-bottom: 0in;">
	<li style=" margin-bottom: 1em;">Governance</li>
	<li style=" margin-bottom: 1em;">Risk management</li>
	<li style=" margin-bottom: 1em;">Internal controls</li>
	<li style=" margin-bottom: 1em;">Capital policy</li>
	<li style=" margin-bottom: 1em;">Scenario design</li>
	<li style=" margin-bottom: 1em;">Projection methodologies</li>
</ul>

<p>Further, the attached guidance includes several appendices that detail supervisory expectations on a firm's capital planning process. This guidance largely consolidates the Federal Reserve's existing capital planning guidance, including:</p>

<ul style=" margin-bottom: 0in;">
	<li style=" margin-bottom: 1em;">Capital Planning at Large Bank Holding Companies: Supervisory Expectations and Range of Current Practice (August 2013)<a href="#fn4" title="footnote 4"><sup>4</sup></a><a id="f4" name="f4"> </a></li>
	<li style=" margin-bottom: 1em;">Comprehensive Capital Analysis and Review 2015 Summary Instructions and Guidance (October 2014)<a href="#fn5" title="footnote 5"><sup>5</sup></a><a id="f5" name="f5"> </a></li>
	<li style=" margin-bottom: 1em;">Instructions for the Capital Assessments and Stress Testing information collection (Reporting Form FR Y-14A), (OMB No. 7100-0341)<a href="#fn6" title="footnote 6"><sup>6</sup></a><a id="f6" name="f6"> </a></li>
	<li style=" margin-bottom: 1em;">SR letter 11-7, "Supervisory Guidance on Model Risk Management"</li>
	<li style=" margin-bottom: 1em;">SR letter 12-7, "Supervisory Guidance on Stress Testing for Banking Organizations with More Than $10 Billion in Total Consolidated Assets"</li>
	<li style=" margin-bottom: 1em;">SR letter 12-17/CA letter 12-14, "Consolidated Supervision Framework for Large Financial Institutions"</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Applicability</strong></p>

<p>This guidance applies to U.S. bank holding companies and intermediate holding companies of foreign banking organizations that have total consolidated assets of at least $50&nbsp;billion but less than $250 billion, have consolidated total on-balance sheet foreign exposure of less than $10 billion, and are not otherwise subject to the Federal Reserve's Large Institution Supervision Coordinating Committee framework (referred to as a "Large and Noncomplex Firm" in this letter and attachment).<a href="#fn7" title="footnote 7"><sup>7</sup></a><a id="f7" name="f7"> </a><sup>,<a href="#fn8" title="footnote 8">8</a><a id="f8" name="f8"> </a></sup>The guidance is effective immediately for bank holding companies that are subject to the capital plan rule as of January 1, 2016. The guidance will become effective for intermediate holding companies beginning on January 1, 2017, which is the date on which the capital plan rule applies to these firms.</p>

<p>The Federal Reserve has different expectations for sound capital planning and capital adequacy depending on the size, scope of operations, activities, and systemic importance of a firm. Concurrently with issuance of this guidance, the Federal Reserve is issuing separate guidance for U.S. bank holding companies and intermediate holding companies that are subject to the LISCC framework (referred to as a "LISCC Firm") or that otherwise have total consolidated assets of $250&nbsp;billion or more or consolidated total on-balance sheet foreign exposure of $10&nbsp;billion or more (referred to as a "Large and Complex Firm"). This separate guidance clarifies that expectations for LISCC Firms and Large and Complex Firms are higher than the expectations for Large and Noncomplex Firms.</p>

<p>Federal Reserve Banks should distribute this letter to Large and Noncomplex Firms in their districts as well as to appropriate supervisory staff. Questions regarding this guidance may be directed to the following Board staff: Lisa&nbsp;Ryu, Associate Director, at <span style="white-space:nowrap">(202) 263-4833</span>, or David&nbsp;Palmer, Senior Supervisory Financial Analyst, at <span style="white-space:nowrap">(202) 452-2904</span>. In addition, questions may be sent to the secure CCAR Communications mailbox or via the Board's public website.<a href="#fn9" title="footnote 9"><sup>9</sup></a><a id="f9" name="f9">&nbsp;</a></p>
