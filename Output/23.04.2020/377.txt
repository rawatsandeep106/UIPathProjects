<td width=88% align=left><font face="times new roman" size=3><b>Uniform Rating System for Information Technology
</b></font></td>
</tr>
</table><br>
<p>
<font size=3 face="times new roman">
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
On January 13, 1999, the Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council (FFIEC) adopted a revised Uniform Rating System for Information Technology (URSIT).<a href="#Footnote1" title="Footnote 1" name="Footref1"><sup>1</sup></a>&nbsp; The FFIEC published the revised rating system in the <u>Federal</u>&nbsp;<u>Register</u> on January&nbsp;20,&nbsp;1999 (64&nbsp;FR&nbsp;3109).&nbsp; The revised URSIT (<a href="sr9908a1.pdf">Attachment 1</a> <font color="gray">- 58&nbsp;KB&nbsp;PDF</font>) becomes effective April&nbsp;1,&nbsp;1999, and is to be used in information technology examinations of all banks and data processing service providers commencing after that date.
<p>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
The banking agencies originally adopted the URSIT on the recommendation of the FFIEC in 1978.&nbsp; Over the years, the URSIT has proven to be an effective internal supervisory tool for evaluating the condition of an institution's or service provider's information technology function.&nbsp; Changes in information technology as well as in the banking agencies' supervisory policies and procedures, prompted a review and revision of the 1978 rating system.&nbsp; In June&nbsp;1998, a proposed revision to the URSIT was issued for public comment and distributed to examiners for field-testing.&nbsp; The final revised URSIT incorporates the comments received on the proposal and from the field testing.&nbsp; The revisions include:
<p>
<ul>
<ul type=disc>
<li>Additional language to conform the URSIT to the Uniform Financial Institution Rating System (UFIRS).<a href="#Footnote2" title="Footnote 2" name="Footref2"><sup>2</sup></a></li>
<p>
<li>Clarification of the component ratings and a reformat of the descriptions for the ratings.<a href="#Footnote3" title="Footnote 3" name="Footref3"><sup>3</sup></a></li>
<p>
<li>Two new component categories -- &quot;Development and Acquisition&quot; and &quot;Support and Delivery&quot; which replace &quot;Systems and Programming&quot; and &quot;Operations.&quot;</li>
<p>
<li>An emphasis on the quality of risk management processes in each of the rating components.</li>
<p>
<li>A requirement that examiners explicitly identify the risk types that are considered in assigning component ratings.</li>
</ul>
</ul>
<p>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
In order to facilitate implementation of the URSIT, a guide adapted from the Information Systems Audit and Control Foundation COBIT Implementation Tool Set is provided in <a href="sr9908a2.pdf">Attachment 2</a> <font color="gray">(37&nbsp;KB&nbsp;PDF)</font>.&nbsp; The implementation guide identifies technology concerns and their relationship to specific rating factors.&nbsp; This guidance provides a risk analysis baseline for the identification of critical areas in a risk-focused examination methodology.
<p>
