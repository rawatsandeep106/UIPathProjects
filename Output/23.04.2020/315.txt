<td width=12% valign=top align=left><b>SUBJECT:</b></td>
<td width=88% align=left><font SIZE=3><b>Combined Examination/Inspection Report For Bank Holding Companies With Lead State Member Banks
</b></font></td>
</tr>
</table><br>
<font SIZE=3><p>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As part of the Federal Reserve's recent review of
examination and supervisory policies and procedures, certain
recommendations for enhancements have been agreed upon by
Board and Reserve Bank senior officers.&nbsp;&nbsp;One of these
recommendations is to permit Reserve Banks, under specific
circumstances, to issue a combined report for a bank holding
company and its lead state member bank subsidiary.<br>
<p>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The combined report may be issued when:<br>
<p>
<ol>
<li>A bank holding company's lead bank subsidiary is a state
member bank<a href="#Footnote1" title="Footnote 1" name="Footref1"><SUP>1</SUP></a>; and <br>
<p>
<li>The holding company's board formally approves a combined
report being released to its lead state member bank
subsidiary. <br>
</ol>
<p>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A letter should be sent to the qualified holding
companies that explains their option of receiving a combined
report.&nbsp;&nbsp;If the holding company's board wishes to receive a
combined report, it should formally approve the release of the
combined report to its lead state member bank subsidiary by
board resolution.<br>
<p>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A combined examination/inspection report format is
attached.&nbsp;&nbsp;At a minimum, a combined report will contain all
examination report pages required by SR 93-71 (final
interagency examination report instructions), as well as
information on the parent company, its subsidiaries, and the
consolidated organization.&nbsp;&nbsp;For detailed information on
required and optional pages, refer to Section 6000 of the
Commercial Bank Examination Manual and Section 5010 of the
Bank Holding Company Supervision Manual.&nbsp;&nbsp;<br>
<p>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Please submit combined reports to:<br>
<p>
<ul>
<ul>
Board of Governors <br>
Division of Banking Supervision and Regulation <br>
Examination Processing <br>
Mail Stop 153 <br>
Surveillance Section <br>
Washington, D.C.&nbsp;&nbsp;20551 <br>
</ul>
</ul>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Separate examination and inspection SIS entries are
required for each combined report.&nbsp;&nbsp;The combined report's
cover page should be light green and provide BHC and Bank RSSD
numbers, as well as a clear indication that the report is a
combined report.<br>
<p>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A shell combined report format is available upon request
(prepared in WordPerfect for Windows, Version 5.2).&nbsp;&nbsp;If you
have any questions regarding the policy outlined above, or you
wish to receive the shell report format, please contact David
Watson at (202)&nbsp;452-3412.<br>
<p>
