<h3>Summary</h3>

<p>The Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC), the Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System, and the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (the agencies) today issued an â€œInteragency Advisory on Use of Evaluations in Real Estate-Related Financial Transactions.â€ This advisory describes existing supervisory expectations, guidance, and industry practice for using evaluations instead of appraisals when estimating the market value of real property securing real estate-related financial transactions. During outreach meetings with representatives from the financial industry conducted pursuant to the requirement of the Economic Growth and Regulatory Paperwork Reduction Act (EGRPRA),<a href="#ft1"><sup>1</sup></a> meeting participants raised questions about the use of evaluations. The questions focused on when evaluations could be used for real estate-related financial transactions and how to support a market value conclusion when there have been few or no recent comparable sales.</p>

<div class="note">
<h3>Note for Community Banks</h3>

<p>This guidance applies to all national banks and federal savings associations engaged in real estate lending.</p>
</div>

<h3>Highlights</h3>

<p>The â€œInteragency Appraisal and Evaluation Guidelinesâ€<a href="#ft2"><sup>2</sup></a> provide guidance on the development and use of evaluations. Transactions that permit use of evaluations include the following:</p>

<ul>
<li>Transactions in which the â€œtransaction valueâ€<a href="#ft3"><sup>3</sup></a> (generally the loan amount) is $250,000 or less.</li>

<li>Certain renewals, refinances, or other transactions involving existing extensions of credit.</li>

<li>Real estate-secured business loans in which the transaction value is $1,000,000 or less and the sale of, or rental income derived from, real estate is not the primary source of repayment for the loan.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Further Information</h3>

<p>Please contact Robert L. Parson, Appraisal Policy Specialist, Credit Risk, at (202) 649-6670.</p>

<p>&#160;</p>

<p>Jennifer C. Kelly<br />
Senior Deputy Comptroller and Chief National Bank Examiner</p>
