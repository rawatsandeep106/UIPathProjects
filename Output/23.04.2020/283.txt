<td width=88% align=left><font SIZE=3><b>Examination and Inspection Reports

<font SIZE=3><p>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Effective immediately, a statement describing the
percentage of loans reviewed in each credit-extending nonbank
subsidiary should be presented on the scope of examination page
of the inspection report.&nbsp;&nbsp;The percentage will represent the
<I>actual</I> loans reviewed by Federal Reserve examiners divided by
total loans in each nonbank subsidiary.&nbsp;&nbsp;Statistical sampling or
any form of extrapolation would not enter into the percentage
derived for this purpose.&nbsp;&nbsp;In situations where different
categories of loans are housed in a nonbank subsidiary, it may be
relevant to present a percentage of loans reviewed by each
different category, or different percentages presented if one
category of loans is actually reviewed and another category is
assessed by delinquency or a delinquency formula method.
<p>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The instructions for the scope of examination page of
the commercial bank examination report presently require a
statement describing the actual percentage of loans reviewed, and
this should continue.&nbsp;&nbsp; However, in state member banks with
assets in excess of $l billion, it would appear appropriate to
present the percentage of loans reviewed in each category.
<p>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Please contact Jim Garner (202-452-2704), if you have
any questions.
