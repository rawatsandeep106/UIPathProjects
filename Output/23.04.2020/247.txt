                <h3 id="title">SR 16-16 / CA 16-7: <p>Special Post-Employment Restriction for Senior Examiners</p></h3>
                
                <div class="row padded-row sr-letter__letterhead">
                	<div class="col-xs-12 col-sm-3">
                		<span class="icon icon__xxl icon-frb-logo-black"></span>
                	</div>
                	<div class="col-xs-12 col-sm-5">
                		BOARD OF GOVERNORS<br/>
                		OF THE FEDERAL RESERVE SYSTEM<br/>
                		WASHINGTON, D.C. 20551
                	</div>
                	<div class="col-xs-12 col-sm-4">
                		<p>
                		DIVISION OF BANKING<br/>
                		SUPERVISION AND REGULATION
                		</p>
    		
                        
                        <p>DIVISION OF CONSUMER<br>AND COMMUNITY AFFAIRS</p>
                            
                		<strong>SR 16-16 / CA 16-7</strong><br/>
                		<strong>November 16, 2016</strong>
                		
                		
                	</div>
                </div>
                
                
                            
                <div class="row padded-row">
                    <div class="col-xs-12col-xs-12 col-md-12 col-sm-12"><strong><p>TO THE OFFICER IN CHARGE OF SUPERVISION AT EACH FEDERAL RESERVE BANK</p></strong></div>
                </div>
                
                <div class="row padded-row sr-letter__subject">
                	<div class="col-xs-12 col-sm-2">SUBJECT:</div>
                	<div class="col-xs-12 col-sm-10"><p>Special Post-Employment Restriction for Senior Examiners</p></div>
                </div>
                
                <div class="row panel panel-default panel-padded">
                	<p><strong>Applicability:</strong>&nbsp; This letter applies to: (1)&nbsp;examiners who have served as a “senior examiner” for a depository institution or depository institution holding company for two or more months during the examiner’s final twelve months of employment with a Reserve Bank, and (2)&nbsp;any examiner or Reserve Bank supervision staff who accepts employment with a depository institution or depository institution holding company that he or she examined in the twelve months prior to his or her departure from the Reserve Bank.</p>
                </div>
                
                <div class="row">
                	<p>This letter is being issued to announce the recent amendment to the Board's rule on <span style="white-space:nowrap">Post-Employment</span> Restrictions for Senior Examiners <span style="white-space:nowrap">(12 CFR 264a)</span> that expands the definition of "senior examiner."&nbsp; This amendment is intended to promote consistency in <span style="white-space:nowrap">post-employment</span> ethics rules across the Federal Reserve System and to address the risk associated with individuals leaving the Federal Reserve for employment with a regulated entity.&nbsp; Further, Reserve Banks are required to notify their employees when they are considered a "senior examiner" and subject to the <span style="white-space:nowrap">post-employment</span> restriction.</p>

<p><strong>Summary of the Special Post-Employment Restriction</strong></p>

<p>In 2005, the federal bank regulatory agencies<a href="#_ftn1" id="_ftnref1" name="_ftnref1" title="Footnote 1"><sup>1</sup></a> issued rules to implement the special <span style="white-space:nowrap">post-employment</span> restriction set forth in the Intelligence Reform and Terrorism Prevention Act of&nbsp;2004 (referred to as the "Act").&nbsp; The special <span style="white-space:nowrap">post-employment</span> restriction set forth in the Act prohibits an examiner who served as a "senior examiner" for a depository institution or depository institution holding company for two or more months during the examiner's final twelve months of employment with a Reserve Bank from knowingly accepting compensation as an employee, officer, director, or consultant from that depository institution or depository institution holding company, or from certain related entities.</p>

<p>For purposes of this rule, an officer or employee of the Federal Reserve is considered to be a "senior examiner" for a particular depository institution or depository institution holding company<a href="#_ftn2" id="_ftnref2" name="_ftnref2" title="Footnote 2"><sup>2</sup></a> if the individual meets all of the following criteria:</p>

<ul>
	<li>The officer or employee has been authorized by the Board to conduct examinations or inspections on behalf of the Board.</li>
	<li style=" margin-top: 2ex;">The officer or employee has been assigned continuing, broad, and lead responsibility for examining or inspecting that depository institution or depository institution holding company.</li>
	<li style=" margin-top: 2ex;">The officer's or employee's responsibilities for examining, inspecting, and supervising the depository institution or depository institution holding company:
	<ul>
		<li style=" margin-top: 2ex;">represent a substantial portion of the officer's or employee's assigned responsibilities, and</li>
		<li style=" margin-top: 2ex;">require the officer or employee to interact routinely with officers or employees of the depository institution or depository institution holding company.</li>
	</ul>
	</li>
</ul>

<p>This rule applies only to an individual serving in a leadership role who is dedicated to supervising a <em>single</em> depository institution (or group of affiliated depository institutions) or depository institution holding company.&nbsp; Specifically, the rule applies to senior supervisory officers&nbsp;(SSOs), deputy SSOs, enterprise risk officers&nbsp;(EROs),<a href="#_ftn3" id="_ftnref3" name="_ftnref3" title="Footnote 3"><sup>3</sup></a> central points of contact&nbsp;(CPCs), deputy CPCs, and supervisory team leaders.<a href="#_ftn4" id="_ftnref4" name="_ftnref4" title="Footnote 4"><sup>4</sup></a></p>

<p>The rule does <em>not</em> cover an individual who:</p>

<ul>
	<li>is dedicated to supervising a single depository institution (or group of affiliated depository institutions) or depository institution holding company, but does not have leadership responsibilities in conjunction with this role;</li>
	<li style=" margin-top: 2ex;">serves in a leadership role for <em>multiple</em> unaffiliated depository institutions or depository institution holding companies at the same time; or</li>
	<li style=" margin-top: 2ex;">performs only periodic, short-term examinations of a depository institution or depository institution holding company, dedicating less than two&nbsp;months in a year to that institution.</li>
</ul>

<p>Table 1 summarizes how the restriction applies to "senior examiners" of the different types of organizations within the Federal Reserve's jurisdiction.</p>

<table border="1" cellpadding="5" style="border-collapse:collapse; width:100%" title="Table 1 - Summary of Prohibited Employment Based on Examination Responsibility">
	<caption style=" font: 12.8px arial, sans-serif; margin-bottom: 1.25em;"><strong>Table 1 – Summary of Prohibited Employment Based on Examination Responsibility</strong></caption>
	<tbody>
		<tr>
			<th bgcolor="#ccc" scope="col" style=" font: 12px arial, sans-serif; text-align: center;"><strong>Senior Examiner Assignment</strong></th>
			<th bgcolor="#ccc" scope="col" style=" font: 12px arial, sans-serif; text-align: center;"><strong>Corresponding Prohibition</strong></th>
		</tr>
		<tr>
			<td scope="row" style=" font: 12px arial, sans-serif; vertical-align: top;"><em>If, during two or more months of your last twelve months of service, you served as a "senior examiner" [for&nbsp;example, as an SSO, deputy SSO, ERO, CPC, deputy CPC, or supervisory team leader] for a:</em></td>
			<td style=" font: 12px arial, sans-serif; vertical-align: top;"><em>Then, for one year after leaving the Federal Reserve System, you may not knowingly accept compensation as an officer, director, employee, or consultant from:</em></td>
		</tr>
		<tr>
			<td scope="row" style=" font: 12px arial, sans-serif; vertical-align: top;">State member bank</td>
			<td style=" font: 12px arial, sans-serif; vertical-align: top;">
			<ul>
				<li>The state member bank (including any subsidiary of the state member bank), or</li>
				<li style=" margin-top: 1.5ex;">Any company (including a bank holding company) that controls the state member bank.</li>
			</ul>
			</td>
		</tr>
		<tr>
			<td scope="row" style=" font: 12px arial, sans-serif; vertical-align: top;">Bank holding company&nbsp;(BHC) or savings and loan holding company&nbsp;(SLHC)</td>
			<td style=" font: 12px arial, sans-serif; vertical-align: top;">
			<ul>
				<li>The BHC or SLHC, or</li>
				<li style=" margin-top: 1.5ex;">Any depository institution controlled by the BHC or SLHC (including any subsidiary of the depository institution).</li>
			</ul>
			</td>
		</tr>
		<tr>
			<td scope="row" style=" font: 12px arial, sans-serif; vertical-align: top;">Foreign bank</td>
			<td style=" font: 12px arial, sans-serif; vertical-align: top;">
			<ul>
				<li>The foreign bank,</li>
				<li style=" margin-top: 1.5ex;">Any U.S. branch or agency of the foreign bank, or</li>
				<li style=" margin-top: 1.5ex;">Any U.S. depository institution controlled by the foreign bank (including any subsidiary of the depository institution).</li>
			</ul>
			</td>
		</tr>
		<tr>
			<td scope="row" style=" font: 12px arial, sans-serif; vertical-align: top;">Financial market utility&nbsp;(FMU)</td>
			<td style=" font: 12px arial, sans-serif; vertical-align: top;">
			<ul>
				<li>The FMU, but only if it is a depository institution or depository institution holding company, or</li>
				<li style=" margin-top: 1.5ex;">Any entity controlled by the FMU, but only if the FMU is a depository institution holding company (including any subsidiary of the entity).</li>
			</ul>
			</td>
		</tr>
		<tr>
			<td scope="row" style=" font: 12px arial, sans-serif; vertical-align: top;">Nonbank financial company&nbsp;(NFC) that is designated by the Financial Stability Oversight Council&nbsp;(FSOC) for supervision by the Federal Reserve</td>
			<td style=" font: 12px arial, sans-serif; vertical-align: top;">
			<ul>
				<li>The NFC if it is a depository institution or depository institution holding company, or</li>
				<li style=" margin-top: 1.5ex;">Any entity controlled by the NFC, but only if the NFC is a depository institution holding company (including any subsidiary of the institution).</li>
			</ul>
			</td>
		</tr>
	</tbody>
</table>

<p><strong>Penalty for Violating "Senior Examiner" Restriction</strong></p>

<p>The restriction applies to a covered individual for one year after the individual terminates his or her employment with the Reserve Bank.&nbsp; If an examiner violates the <span style="white-space:nowrap">one-year</span> restriction, the statute requires the appropriate federal bank regulatory agency to seek an order of removal and <span style="white-space:nowrap">industry-wide</span> employment prohibition for up to five&nbsp;years, a civil money penalty of up to $250,000, or both.&nbsp; In special circumstances, the Chairman of the Board of Governors may waive the restriction for a "senior examiner" of the Federal Reserve by certifying in writing that granting the individual a waiver of the restriction would not affect the integrity of the Federal Reserve's supervisory program.</p>

<p><strong>Administrative Procedures for Implementing the "Senior Examiner" Restriction and Additional Guidelines</strong></p>

<p>At a minimum, Reserve Banks shall adopt the following procedures to ensure that the "senior examiner" rule is properly implemented:</p>

<div style=" margin-left: 30px;">
<p><strong>Notification to senior examiners:</strong>&nbsp; To help examiners comply with the statute, Reserve Banks shall establish procedures to periodically and regularly review examiners' duties and promptly notify examiners in writing when a change in duties would cause an examiner to be considered a "senior examiner" or cease to be considered a "senior examiner" with respect to an institution or holding company for purposes of the rule.&nbsp; Reserve Banks should consult with Board staff if questions arise as to whether an examiner would be considered a "senior examiner."&nbsp; The attachment to this letter provides a sample form for a Notice of Post‑Employment Restriction that Reserve Banks can use for such notification.</p>

<p><strong>Examiners' responsibility:</strong>&nbsp; Examiners are responsible for becoming familiar with the rule and ensuring that they comply with the rule.&nbsp; Examiners should direct any questions they may have regarding the rule to the Reserve Bank's designated ethics officers.</p>

<p><strong>Monitoring of senior examiner assignments.</strong>&nbsp; Reserve Banks shall maintain electronic records of examiners covered by the rule.&nbsp; These records at a minimum shall include:</p>

<ul>
	<li>the name of each "senior examiner;"</li>
	<li style=" margin-top: 2ex;">the name of the depository institution or depository institution holding company for which the examiner is considered a "senior examiner;"</li>
	<li style=" margin-top: 2ex;">the duration of the examiner's service as a "senior examiner" for the depository institution or depository institution holding company; and</li>
	<li style=" margin-top: 2ex;">if the "senior examiner" ends employment with the Reserve Bank, the last date of Reserve Bank employment, the reason for leaving, and the name of the organization with which the examiner has accepted employment, if available.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Work Paper review:</strong>&nbsp; If <strong><em>any</em></strong> examiner, regardless of whether he or she is designated as a "senior examiner," accepts employment with a depository institution or depository institution holding company that he or she examined in the twelve months prior to his or her departure from Federal Reserve employment, the Reserve Bank shall review the work papers related to his or her assignment supervising that institution.&nbsp; The work paper review should be performed within 60&nbsp;days of the examiner's departure and should consider whether the examiner compromised examination findings or supervisory proceedings because of pending employment with the relevant depository institution or depository institution holding company (for example, the examiner failed to bring significant findings or concerns forward to examination management, or omitted important examination processes or elements of the examination scope).</p>

<p><strong>Disciplinary procedures:</strong>&nbsp; If a Reserve Bank becomes aware that a former examiner has violated the rule, the Reserve Bank shall promptly notify the Reserve Bank's <span style="white-space:nowrap">officer-in-charge</span> of supervision, its ethics officer, and the Board's designated agency ethics officer.</p>
</div>

<p><strong>Implementation</strong></p>

<p>Reserve Banks are expected to implement this policy within 45&nbsp;days from the date of this letter.&nbsp; In early&nbsp;2017, a horizontal review will be conducted by each Reserve Bank's Quality Assurance function, in partnership with Board staff, to ensure that Reserve Banks have effectively implemented the new policy.&nbsp; In addition, Board staff may review compliance with this policy during the operations review program and may request local Reserve Bank departments, such as a Reserve Bank's Internal Audit function, to assist in validating compliance.</p>
