                <h3 id="title">SR 15-8: <p>Name Check Process for Domestic and International Applications</p></h3>
                
                <div class="row padded-row sr-letter__letterhead">
                	<div class="col-xs-12 col-sm-3">
                		<span class="icon icon__xxl icon-frb-logo-black"></span>
                	</div>
                	<div class="col-xs-12 col-sm-5">
                		BOARD OF GOVERNORS<br/>
                		OF THE FEDERAL RESERVE SYSTEM<br/>
                		WASHINGTON, D.C. 20551
                	</div>
                	<div class="col-xs-12 col-sm-4">
                		<p>
                		DIVISION OF BANKING<br/>
                		SUPERVISION AND REGULATION
                		</p>
    		
                        
                		<strong>SR 15-8</strong><br/>
                		<strong>June 25, 2015</strong>
                		
                		
                	</div>
                </div>
                
                
                            
                <div class="row padded-row">
                    <div class="col-xs-12col-xs-12 col-md-12 col-sm-12"><strong><p>TO THE OFFICER IN CHARGE OF SUPERVISION AT EACH FEDERAL RESERVE BANK</p></strong></div>
                </div>
                
                <div class="row padded-row sr-letter__subject">
                	<div class="col-xs-12 col-sm-2">SUBJECT:</div>
                	<div class="col-xs-12 col-sm-10"><p>Name Check Process for Domestic and International Applications</p></div>
                </div>
                
                <div class="row panel panel-default panel-padded">
                	<p><strong>Applicability:</strong>&nbsp; This guidance applies to all financial institutions supervised by the Federal Reserve, including those with $10&nbsp;billion or less in consolidated assets.</p>
                </div>
                
                <div class="row">
                	<p><strong>Introduction</strong></p>

<p>As part of the Federal Reserve’s responsibilities as the primary federal banking regulator for bank holding companies, savings and loan holding companies, and <span style="white-space:nowrap">state-chartered</span> banks that are members of the Federal Reserve System (referred to as “supervised financial institutions” <a href="#_ftn1" id="_ftnref1" name="_ftnref1" title="Footnote 1"><sup>1</sup></a>), the Federal Reserve reviews applications and notices (collectively, “applications”) that may include changes to the ownership and/or the composition of the board of directors or executive management of a supervised financial institution.&nbsp; For many of these applications, the Federal Reserve’s review includes an assessment of whether certain proposed shareholders and policymakers have the competence, experience, integrity, character, and financial resources to effectively lead a supervised financial institution in a <span style="white-space:nowrap">safe-and-sound manner.<a href="#_ftn2" id="_ftnref2" name="_ftnref2" title="Footnote 2"><sup>2</sup></a></span></p>

<p>Under certain circumstances, the Federal Reserve also requests from other regulatory and investigative agencies background information about an individual or company involved in a proposal; this is commonly referred to as the “name check” process.<a href="#_ftn3" id="_ftnref3" name="_ftnref3" title="Footnote 3"><sup>3</sup></a>&nbsp; This letter describes the Federal Reserve’s general name check process and the changes that are being implemented to streamline and simplify the process and, thereby, help to reduce the burden associated with the application process overall, particularly for community banking organizations.<a href="#_ftn4" id="_ftnref4" name="_ftnref4" title="Footnote 4"><sup>4</sup></a></p>

<p><strong>Previous Name Check Process</strong></p>

<p>Under the previous process, name checks generally were conducted on all proposed officers and directors and/or new principal shareholders of a supervised financial institution involved in an application under consideration by the Federal Reserve.<a href="#_ftn5" id="_ftnref5" name="_ftnref5" title="Footnote 5"><sup>5</sup></a>&nbsp;&nbsp;Exceptions were made for individuals considered “known to banking”<a href="#_ftn6" id="_ftnref6" name="_ftnref6" title="Footnote 6"><sup>6</sup></a> and proposed outside directors with limited or no ownership interests (that is, less than <span style="white-space:nowrap">five percent)</span> in the supervised financial institution.&nbsp; Where the specific facts and circumstances warrant, name checks were conducted on an entire board or ownership group.&nbsp; For example, in proposals which involved numerous organizers (each with limited or no ownership in the relevant supervised financial institution) and no clear top policymakers, name checks generally were conducted for the entire group of organizers.</p>

<p><strong>New Name Check Process</strong></p>

<p>The Federal Reserve is implementing several changes to the name check process.&nbsp; The Federal Reserve generally will conduct name checks only on an individual that, upon consummation of an application, will become a principal shareholder or one of the top two policymakers of the supervised financial institution.<a href="#_ftn7" id="_ftnref7" name="_ftnref7" title="Footnote 7"><sup>7</sup></a>&nbsp; In addition, the Federal Reserve will no longer take into consideration whether an individual is “known to banking” when determining whether a name check must be conducted.&nbsp; Rather, unless the facts and circumstances suggest otherwise, a completed name check will remain current for a period of <span style="white-space:nowrap">five years,</span> and individuals and companies with current name checks will generally not be rechecked, unless circumstances indicate to the Reserve Bank or Board staff that a name check is appropriate.<a href="#_ftn8" id="_ftnref8" name="_ftnref8" title="Footnote 8"><sup>8</sup></a></p>

<p>In addition to the above changes, the Federal Reserve will obtain credit bureau reports in certain limited situations to supplement and corroborate financial information provided in application filings or from other sources.&nbsp; The use of such “credit checks” will align our practice with that of other federal banking agencies.&nbsp; These credit checks will be conducted on an ad hoc basis when the facts and circumstance indicate that the information provided in the credit report could be helpful to the Federal Reserve in its comprehensive assessment of individuals under review.</p>

<p>Questions with respect to policy matters discussed in this letter should be directed to the following staff in the Board’s Domestic/International Banking Acquisition and Activities section:&nbsp; <span style="white-space:nowrap">Susan Motyka,</span> Manager, at <span style="white-space:nowrap">(202) 452-5280;</span> or <span style="white-space:nowrap">Patrick Grant,</span> Senior Supervisory Financial Analyst, at <span style="white-space:nowrap">(202) 452-5214.</span>&nbsp; Questions concerning the general process for conducting name checks can be directed to <span style="white-space:nowrap">Susan Motyka;</span> <span style="white-space:nowrap">Patrick Grant;</span> <span style="white-space:nowrap">Katie Cox,</span> Manager, at <span style="white-space:nowrap">(202) 452-2721;</span> or <span style="white-space:nowrap">Betsy Howes-Bean,</span> Manager, at <span style="white-space:nowrap">(202) 452-3096.</span>&nbsp; In addition, institutions may send questions via the Board’s public website.<a href="#_ftn9" id="_ftnref9" name="_ftnref9" title="Footnote 9"><sup>9</sup></a></p>
                </div>
